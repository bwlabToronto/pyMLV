\hypertarget{index_intro}{}\doxysection{\texorpdfstring{Introduction}{Introduction}}\label{index_intro}
py\+MLV is the Python version of the Mid-\/level Vision Toolbox (MLVToolbox), created by the Aravind Narayanan in BWLab at the University of Toronto. The toolbox facilitates the extraction and analysis of structural properties in images such as contours\textquotesingle{} orientation, length, curvature, and junctions. It supports a variety of research tasks including the identification of perceptual organization cues like mirror symmetry, ribbon symmetry, and taper symmetry in naturalistic images.\hypertarget{index_usage}{}\doxysection{\texorpdfstring{Usage}{Usage}}\label{index_usage}
To get started, clone the repository and navigate to the demos folder. You can execute the Jupyter notebooks within to see examples of the toolbox\textquotesingle{}s capabilities in action\+:
\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item {\bfseries{Demo\+\_\+\+Contour\+Features}}\+: Demonstrates extraction of contour features.
\item {\bfseries{Demo\+\_\+\+Medial\+Axis}}\+: Shows how to compute and visualize the medial axis of images. Note that the medial axis demo requires additional components due to MATLAB dependencies.
\end{DoxyItemize}\hypertarget{index_updates}{}\doxysection{\texorpdfstring{Updates and Development}{Updates and Development}}\label{index_updates}
